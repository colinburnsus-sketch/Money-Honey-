Common fees
•	Annual fee: ranges from $0 to $895 on major cards; premium travel cards often sit at $325, $395, $795, or $895.[bankrate +2]
•	Balance transfer fee: typically 3% to 5% of the amount transferred.[money.usnews]
•	Cash advance fee: typically 2% to 5% of the cash borrowed.[money.usnews]
•	Foreign transaction fee: typically 1% to 3%.[money.usnews]
•	Late payment fee: commonly varies by issuer, with recent guidance noting the CFPB’s attempted cap at $8 was later withdrawn.[money.usnews]
•	Returned payment fee: commonly around $35.[money.usnews]

Practical read
The main pattern is that higher-fee cards usually pair those fees with travel credits, lounge access, or bonus categories, while no-fee cards rely more on straightforward cash back. For many people, the right question is whether the credits you’ll actually use are worth more than the annual fee.[bankrate +1]
Best-fit examples
•	If you want no annual fee, the Chase Freedom Unlimited and Citi Double Cash are strong examples of cards with useful ongoing rewards.[bankrate]
•	If you travel often and can fully use the credits, premium cards like the Venture X, Sapphire Reserve, and Amex Platinum can offset large fees.[bankrate]
A quick rule of thumb: if you won’t use at least most of the stated credits, a lower-fee or no-fee card usually makes more sense.[money.usnews +1]
